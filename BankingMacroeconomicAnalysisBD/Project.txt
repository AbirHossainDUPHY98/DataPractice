PostgreSQL by default tries to create a lock/socket file in /run/postgresql, which doesn’t exist or isn’t writable in your setup. Since Nix doesn’t create system directories for services, you need to tell Postgres to use a different Unix socket directory inside your home folder.
Step 1 — Create a socket directory in your home
mkdir -p ~/pgsocket
*** Step 2 — Start Postgres using that socket
pg_ctl -D ~/pgdata -l ~/pgdata/logfile -o "-k ~/pgsocket" start
Explanation:
-o "-k ~/pgsocket" → tells Postgres to put its socket in ~/pgsocket instead of /run/postgresql.
All files (lock and socket) will now live in a writable directory.
Step 3 — Connect with psql
You must tell psql where the socket is:
psql -h ~/pgsocket -d postgres
-h ~/pgsocket → use that socket directory
-d postgres → default database
Step 4 — Create your database
Inside psql:
CREATE DATABASE "BD_Banking_Analysis";
\q
Step 5 — Run your schema
psql -h ~/pgsocket -d BD_Banking_Analysis -f sql/schema.sql
Now it should succeed, creating:
dim_time
dim_indicator
dim_source
fact_macro
fact_banking

# Macro Indicators:
GDP / Output --	GDP, GdpPerCapita, GDPPerCapitaCurrent$
Credit / Money --	BroadMoneyOfGDP, DomesticCreditToPrivateSectorOfGDP
Fiscal --	GovtDebt, TotalInvestment
Prices --	InflationConsumerPricesAnnual, InflationRate
Labor --	UnemployedRate
Banking-related macro --  bankNonperformingLoansToTotalGrossLoans
# Banking Indicators:
Liquidity / Leverage --	depositsToLoansPCT, LoansToDomesticEconomy
Credit --	loansTotal
Stability --  NonperformingLoansPCT

# Macro Calculations
GDP growth: (GDP_this_year − GDP_last_year) / GDP_last_year * 100
GDP per capita growth: same as above, per capita
Broad money / GDP trend: % of GDP over time
Domestic credit / private sector / GDP: monitor financial deepening
Government debt / GDP: fiscal leverage
Inflation trends: CPI or annual inflation rate
Unemployment trends
# Banking Calculations
Loan-to-deposit ratio: loansTotal / deposits or depositsToLoansPCT
Loans to domestic economy: track growth / share
NPL ratio: NonperformingLoansPCT → banking stability
# Cross-calculations (Macro ↔ Banking)
Credit-to-GDP ratio: DomesticCreditToPrivateSectorOfGDP or loansTotal / GDP
NPL-to-GDP trend: NPL ratio × total loans / GDP
Financial depth vs GDP per capita: see if credit growth matches economic growth

## Three Layers of Analysis
 # Macro Trends (economy-wide)
    Purpose: show how Bangladesh’s economy has evolved over time.
    Indicators:GDP, GDP per capita, GDP growth,
               InflationConsumerPricesAnnual, InflationRate
               GovtDebt, TotalInvestment
               UnemployedRate
               BroadMoneyOfGDP, DomesticCreditToPrivateSectorOfGDP
    Calculations:Year-over-year growth rates
                 Trend lines (GDP, per capita income, inflation)
                 Ratios like BroadMoney/GDP, GovtDebt/GDP
                 Reporting / Insights:
                 Identify periods of fast/slow growth
                 Highlight inflation spikes
                 Show fiscal leverage trends
                 Discuss monetary expansion (BroadMoney/GDP)
 # Banking Trends (sector-specific)
    Purpose: evaluate banking sector performance and stability.
    Indicators:loansTotal, LoansToDomesticEconomy
               depositsToLoansPCT
               NonperformingLoansPCT
    Calculations:Loan-to-deposit ratio (liquidity)
                 Credit growth trends
                 NPL ratio trend (banking stability)
    Reporting / Insights:Identify periods of rapid credit growth
                         Highlight risk trends (NPL spikes)
                         Compare loan/deposit ratios to benchmarks
                         Discuss banking sector resilience

 # Macro ↔ Banking (cross-sector)
    Purpose:connect banking activity with the overall economy.
    Examples:Credit-to-GDP ratio → shows financial deepening
             NPL-to-GDP → links banking stress to economic size
             Lending vs GDP growth → real sector impact
             Real lending rate = lending rate − inflation
    Calculations:Ratios and derived metrics using fact_macro + fact_banking
                 Time series trends
                 Growth or volatility measures
    Reporting / Insights:Evaluate if banking growth aligns with GDP growth=
                         Highlight systemic risk periods
                         Identify gaps in credit allocation
                         Recommend policy focus areas

# Decision Logic / Use Cases:
GDP growth --	Macro policy adjustment, investment focus
Inflation -- 	Monetary policy / interest rate guidance
Credit-to-GDP --	Financial deepening assessment
NPL ratio --	Banking sector health; stricter supervision if high
Loan-to-deposit ratio --	Liquidity risk; capital requirement check
Real lending rate --	Encourage productive lending; curb speculative lending
